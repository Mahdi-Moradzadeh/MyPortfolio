Education

Masters of Applied Sciences

During the completion of my masters degree, I had focused on a rare condition that occurs to concrete structures of basement walls and foundations. The reason for such phenomenons are found to be originated from presence of sulphate in aggregates within the concrete matrix or scientifically known as Internal Sulphate attack (a.k.a. ISA). Due to lack of information regarding the stated topic, I was tasked to attemp in finding an accelerated method that can identify adequacy of aggregates used in concrete to remain uneffected under extreme conditions.


I have succesfully discovered two new revolutionary methods that could possibly define the adequacy of aggregates to be used by the industry. This ground breaking discovery is currnetly under further investigation to be published and presented to the Canadian Standard Association of concrete structures or CSA as the possible examination process in a universal scale.
